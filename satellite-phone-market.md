<p><p>The satellite phone market comprises mainly two types: Geosynchronous Satellite Phones and Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) Satellite Phones. Geosynchronous satellite phones operate using satellites positioned in a stable orbit about 35,786 kilometers above Earth, ensuring consistent coverage but higher latency. In contrast, LEO satellite phones utilize a network of satellites orbiting closer to Earth, typically around 1,200 kilometers, offering lower latency and improved signal quality. Each type caters to specific user needs based on coverage, latency, and operational requirements.</p></p>
