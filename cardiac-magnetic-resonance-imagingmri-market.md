<p><p>Cardiac Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a non-invasive imaging technique used to obtain detailed images of the heart's structure and function. The market is divided into two main types: closed systems and open systems. Closed systems involve traditional MRI machines that provide high-resolution images in a confined space, often resulting in better image quality. Open systems, on the other hand, offer a more spacious environment, reducing patient anxiety but generally yielding lower image resolution. Each type serves distinct patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
