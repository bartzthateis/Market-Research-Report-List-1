<p><p>Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene (BOPP) is a versatile plastic film widely used in packaging, labels, and various applications due to its excellent clarity, strength, and barrier properties. The Tubular Quench Method involves extruding polypropylene into a tube, subsequently biaxially stretching to enhance film properties, ideal for flexible packaging. In contrast, the Flat Film Strength Method produces BOPP films through a flat extrusion process, offering robust mechanical strength and improved dimensional stability, suitable for applications requiring higher rigidity and resistance. Both methods cater to diverse market needs.</p></p>
