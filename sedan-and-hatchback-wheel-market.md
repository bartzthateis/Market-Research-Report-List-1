<p><p>The sedan and hatchback wheel market encompasses two primary manufacturing processes: casting and forging. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold, resulting in complex shapes at lower costs, making it suitable for high-volume production. In contrast, forging involves shaping metal through compressive forces, producing stronger and more durable wheels, ideal for performance vehicles. Both methods cater to diverse consumer preferences, with casting appealing to budget-conscious buyers and forging attracting those prioritizing strength and performance in their sedan or hatchback vehicles.</p></p>
