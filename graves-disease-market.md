<p><p>Graves' disease market consists of several treatment categories aimed at managing hyperthyroidism. Anti-thyroid drugs, such as methimazole and propylthiouracil, inhibit thyroid hormone production. Iodine and iodides, including potassium iodide, reduce hormone release and are often used pre-surgery. Radioactive iodine therapy targets and destroys overactive thyroid cells. Lastly, thyroid hormone synthesis inhibitors, like beta-blockers, alleviate symptoms by blocking hormone effects. Together, these treatments provide a comprehensive approach to managing Graves' disease and its symptoms effectively.</p></p>
